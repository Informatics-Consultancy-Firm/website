ICF-SL possesses specialized expertise in health informatics, DHIS2 implementation, malaria epidemiology, data science, and the development of digital health solutions tailored to the needs of National Malaria Programmes and other health sector stakeholders.

Our multidisciplinary team of statisticians, data scientists, epidemiologists, and software engineers delivers cutting-edge solutions that drive evidence-based decision-making across Sierra Leone and West Africa.

2023

School-Based Distribution of Insecticide-Treated Nets (ITNs)

Project Overview

Implemented the School-Based Distribution (SBD) of ITN campaign in Kono District during 2023. This initiative leveraged the school system as a distribution channel to provide insecticide-treated bed nets to school-age children and their households, a critical malaria prevention strategy aligned with WHO recommendations for continuous malaria control in endemic regions.

ICF-SL's Role

In collaboration with NMCP, ICF-SL developed and deployed the complete digital ecosystem for the SBD campaign:

  • Designed and built the ICF Collect PWA (Progressive Web Application) for real-time data collection from schools
  • Created the ITN Movement App for tracking ITN distribution, stock management, and beneficiary data
  • Implemented QR-based school tracking system for verification and accountability
  • Developed staff ID card systems for field team authentication and monitoring
  • Provided data quality assurance and analytics support throughout the campaign

Mass Insecticide-Treated Nets Distribution Campaign

Project Overview

Executed large-scale mass ITN distribution campaigns in Bo District (2024) and Bombali District (2025), as well as Port Loko, Koinadugu, Pujehun, and Bonthe districts. These mass campaigns represent a critical malaria prevention strategy targeting universal coverage of vulnerable populations with insecticide-treated bed nets.

ICF-SL's Role

ICF-SL provided comprehensive technical support to NMCP for campaign execution and monitoring:

  • Deployed enhanced ITN Movement App with real-time distribution tracking
  • Conducted data quality monitoring and validation during campaign operations
  • Provided daily analytics dashboards for campaign performance tracking
  • Implemented geographic information systems (GIS) for distribution hotspot analysis
  • Developed compliance and accountability reporting systems for district teams

Therapeutic Efficacy Study (TES) Implementation

Project Overview

Supporting the 2026 Therapeutic Efficacy Study, a critical surveillance activity to monitor antimalarial drug effectiveness in Sierra Leone. This study evaluates the efficacy of artemisinin-based combination therapies (ACTs) and other antimalarials through standardized clinical protocols and laboratory testing.

ICF-SL's Role

ICF-SL developed digital tools and analytical support systems for the TES:

  • Built customized TES case record form (CRF) collection tools integrated with offline capability
  • Created automated data validation and quality control workflows
  • Developed clinical outcome tracking dashboards for real-time monitoring
  • Provided statistical analysis and reporting on drug efficacy results
  • Ensured data security and compliance with research protocols

2026

National Malaria Data Repository (NMDR) Establishment

Project Overview

Setting up the National Malaria Data Repository (NMDR) on DHIS2 platform, a centralized data warehouse for comprehensive malaria surveillance and analytics. The NMDR consolidates data from routine health information systems, disease surveillance, intervention tracking, and research studies to support evidence-based malaria control programming.

ICF-SL's Role

ICF-SL is the principal technical partner implementing the NMDR infrastructure:

  • Architected the DHIS2-based data repository with scalable infrastructure
  • Configured metadata, data elements, indicators, and dashboards for malaria analytics
  • Built data integration pipelines from multiple source systems into the NMDR
  • Developed advanced analytics dashboards for program monitoring and evaluation
  • Established data governance frameworks and access controls

2026

MOCEM Application Development in DHIS2

Project Overview

Developing the MOCEM (Mobile Central Epidemiology Management) application within DHIS2 platform to provide real-time disease surveillance and epidemiological monitoring at all administrative levels. MOCEM enables field epidemiologists and program managers to monitor disease trends, investigate outbreaks, and respond promptly to emerging health threats.

ICF-SL's Role

ICF-SL serves as the lead developer for the MOCEM application:

  • Designed and built custom DHIS2 widgets and applications for epidemiological monitoring
  • Created mobile-first interfaces for field surveillance teams
  • Implemented real-time data validation and alert mechanisms for outbreak detection
  • Developed geospatial visualization tools for disease distribution mapping
  • Built integration with case management and response tracking systems

2025

Sub-National Tailoring (SNT) Analytics Support

Project Overview

Providing comprehensive data analytics support to NMCP for Sub-National Tailoring (SNT) of malaria interventions in 2025. SNT is a strategic approach to customize malaria prevention and control interventions based on local epidemiology, ecology, and operational capacity to maximize program impact and efficiency.

ICF-SL's Role

ICF-SL provides analytical and technical support for SNT implementation:

  • Conducted epidemiological stratification and risk mapping for all districts
  • Analyzed intervention effectiveness and coverage data by subnational unit
  • Provided decision-support analytics for intervention selection and targeting
  • Developed costing models for intervention packages at district level
  • Created monitoring dashboards for SNT implementation tracking and evaluation
